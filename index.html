Research Experience

Brown University, Computational Biology Lab
Dr. Ritambhara Singh, Jan. 2020 - Present
I am currently doing Computational Biology research in Dr. Singh's lab at Brown. I am working on a project to predict compartments from Hi-C contact matrices in the human genome. I am using machine learning techniques to predict Hi-C compartments from DNase hypersensitivity data, DNA methylation data and histone modification signal data.

University of Massachusetts - Amherst, Food Science Lab
Dr. Yeonhwa Park, July 2018 - Aug. 2018
The goal of my project under Dr. Park was to determine the effects of Sulforaphane, a compound found in cruciferous vegetables, on aging, obesity and oxidative stress in Caenorhabditis elegans. The results indicated that Sulforaphane may improve oxidative stress response in C. elegans through the post-translational regulation of SKN-1. I received awards for both my poster presentation and paper at the Twin Cities Regional Science Fair and Minnesota State Science Fair. I also qualified as a finalist for the prestigious Intel International Science and Engineering Fair, which I attended in May 2019. Through this project, I made extensive use of different data analysis processes using Excel, Statistical Analysis Software (one-way ANOVA and chi-square test), GraphPad Prism (Logrank test) and SPSS.

University of Minnesota, Immunology Lab
Dr. Stephen Jameson, June 2017 - Sept. 2017
In Dr. Jameson's lab, I assisted a postdoctoral student with her research, which was studying killer cells of the adaptive immune system (CD8 T cells) and how to tweak the CD8 T cell response to generate a response against infections and cancer. I became familiar with many wet lab methodologies, such as running PCR on mice blood, sorting data in a Flow Cytometer and analyzing data using the comparative Ct method.
