Research Experience

Research Assistant at the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard
Dr. Neriman Tokcan, June 2021 - Present
I was selected for the prestigious Broad Summer Research Program (2021), where I worked in the Golub and Uhler lab under the mentorship of Neriman Tokcan, Ph.D. My project is focusing on the applications of machine and deep learning methods to spatial transcriptomic data so we can better learn how Classical Hodgkin's Lymphoma tumor cells survive based on interactions they have with nonmalignant cells in the tumor microenvironment. I am using primarily Python and R for this research project.

Brown University, Computational Biology Lab
Dr. Ritambhara Singh, Jan. 2020 - Present
I am currently conducting Computational Biology research in Dr. Singh's lab at Brown. I am working on a project to predict interactions (A/B compartments) between genes from histone modification signal data using regression and classification algorithms. This project is to be presented at the ENCODE project consortium. I am reproducing and expanding on previous results to derive compartments from Hi-C data using Python, R, and Git.

University of Massachusetts - Amherst, Food Science Lab
Dr. Yeonhwa Park, July 2018 - Aug. 2018
The goal of my project under Dr. Park was to determine the effects of Sulforaphane, a compound found in cruciferous vegetables, on aging, obesity and oxidative stress in Caenorhabditis elegans. The results indicated that Sulforaphane may improve oxidative stress response in C. elegans through the post-translational regulation of SKN-1. I received awards for both my poster presentation and paper at the Twin Cities Regional Science Fair and Minnesota State Science Fair. I also qualified as a finalist for the prestigious Intel International Science and Engineering Fair, which I attended in May 2019. Through this project, I made extensive use of different data analysis processes using Excel, Statistical Analysis Software (one-way ANOVA and chi-square test), GraphPad Prism (Logrank test) and SPSS.
